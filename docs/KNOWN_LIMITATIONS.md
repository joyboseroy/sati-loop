# Known Unknowns and Limitations

Honest acknowledgment of limitations is part of the project's intellectual integrity.

## Current limitations (v1.1)

**Classifier reliability is unvalidated.** The Random Forest parameters and threshold values are reasonable initial heuristics, not empirically validated parameters.

**Phenomenological label quality is uncertain.** Beginners frequently misidentify subtle laxity as settled awareness. Training labels from beginners will have systematic biases that the classifier inherits.

**Transfer is hypothesised, not demonstrated.** The central claim — that scaffold withdrawal produces transferable metacognitive skill — has not been demonstrated in a controlled trial with this specific architecture.

**Individual variability is high.** EEG signatures of mind-wandering are highly individual, affected by sleep quality, medication, emotional state, and practice history.

**Cue disruption risk is unquantified.** A cue delivered during genuine settling could disrupt a fragile state. The 3-second confirmation window reduces false positives but does not eliminate them.

**Subtractive cue design is unvalidated.** The preference for subtractive high-frequency roll-off over an additive tone is theoretically motivated but has not been tested in user studies.

**Long-term scaffold withdrawal effects are unknown.** Whether the withdrawal schedule prevents dependency in practice is not yet known.

## What to do with these limitations

Treat Sati-Loop as an experimental research platform. Run the transfer test. Report results including failures. A failed transfer test is a scientifically useful result.
