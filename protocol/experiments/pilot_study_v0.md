# Pilot Study v0 — Design

## Status

Not yet conducted. This document outlines the intended first human pilot.

## Objective

Test whether the Sati-Loop system produces measurable reduction in Delta t (distraction onset to self-correction interval) that transfers to unassisted practice.

## Design

- Single-site, randomised, sham-controlled
- n = 20 (10 active, 10 sham)
- 10-week protocol (Phase A + B + C)
- Participants: meditation practitioners with 1+ years experience

## Primary outcome

Delta t in Phase C (device absent, weeks 9-10) vs Phase A baseline.

## Control condition

Sham device: delivers cues at random intervals uncorrelated with distraction detection. Participants cannot distinguish sham from active device.

## Inclusion criteria

- Age 18-65
- 1+ years regular meditation practice
- No neurological conditions, seizure history, or implanted devices

## Exclusion criteria

- Cardiac pacemaker or implanted neural device
- Pregnancy
- Active seizure disorder
- Current psychosis or dissociative disorder
- Active participation in another meditation-related study

## Ethics

IRB approval required before participant recruitment. Informed consent document to be developed.

## Notes

This is a design document only. No participants have been recruited. No data has been collected.
