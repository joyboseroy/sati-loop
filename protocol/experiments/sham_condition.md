# Sham Condition Specification

## Purpose

The sham device controls for expectation effects, interruption effects, and general attentional priming that any cueing system might produce regardless of alignment with actual distraction.

## Design

The sham device is identical in appearance and hardware to the active device. The only difference is the cue delivery logic:

- **Active device:** cue fires when P(wandering) > threshold for more than tau seconds
- **Sham device:** cue fires at random intervals drawn from the same distribution as the active device's observed cue rate

## Blinding

Participants are told they are receiving a device that "may or may not be calibrated to your specific brain signals." They are not told which condition they are in.

Researchers conducting data collection are also blinded to condition. Only the data analyst and the PI know the allocation.

## Sham cue rate

The sham cue rate is matched to the active group's observed mean cue rate from Phase A. This prevents participants from distinguishing conditions by cue frequency.

## Validation

A successful sham manipulation check: participants cannot identify their condition at a rate better than chance when asked at the end of the study.

## Limitations

This design controls for interruption and expectation but cannot fully control for the effects of EEG awareness (knowing you are being monitored). Future studies may include a passive monitoring control arm.
