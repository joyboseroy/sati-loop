# Label Quality

Phenomenological labels are inherently noisy.

Sati-Loop treats all subjective labels as:
- probabilistic,
- participant-dependent,
- and potentially biased.

---

# Known Issues

## Beginner Misclassification

Beginners frequently confuse:
- subtle laxity,
- dissociation,
- and attentional dullness

with:
- stable attention.

---

## Retrospective Reconstruction

Self-reports may reflect:
- post-hoc interpretation,
- narrative smoothing,
- memory distortion.

---

## Probe Reactivity

Frequent probes may:
- alter practice,
- increase meta-monitoring,
- shift attentional strategy.

---

# Current Design Response

The system:
- avoids continuous self-reporting,
- uses sparse calibration probes,
- prioritizes longitudinal consistency over single labels.

---

# Future Directions

Potential future approaches:
- confidence-weighted labels,
- multi-session calibration,
- teacher-assisted annotation,
- semi-supervised clustering.

---

# Research Position

Phenomenological uncertainty is treated as a central architectural constraint rather than a nuisance variable to eliminate.
