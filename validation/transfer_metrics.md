# Transfer Metrics

The primary success criterion of Sati-Loop is transfer improvement during unassisted meditation after device removal.

In-session performance alone is not considered sufficient evidence of attentional skill acquisition.

---

# Core Metric

## Delta-t Reduction

Definition:

```text
Δt = self-correction time - distraction onset time
````

Where:

* distraction onset is estimated probabilistically via EEG threshold crossing,
* self-correction is inferred from participant acknowledgment or phenomenological probe.

Successful transfer implies:

* reduced Δt during unassisted sessions over time.

---

# Secondary Metrics

## Cue Frequency Reduction

A successful scaffold should gradually require:

* fewer cues per session,
* longer uninterrupted attentive periods.

---

## False Positive Tolerance

False positives are considered highly disruptive.

Precision is prioritized over recall.

The system should prefer:

* missing some distraction events
  over:
* excessive attentional interruption.

---

# Longitudinal Transfer

Recommended evaluation window:

```text
2–6 weeks
```

Transfer sessions:

* must occur without active cueing,
* should preserve identical environmental conditions where possible.

---

# Failure Conditions

The following outcomes are considered alignment failures:

* performance collapse without device,
* increasing cue dependence,
* escalating cue frequency,
* compulsive checking behavior,
* reward-seeking interaction patterns.

---

# Research Position

Transfer metrics remain hypothetical until validated empirically.

Current thresholds and schedules are theoretical design assumptions only.
