# Enclosure and Ergonomic Notes

## Form factor target

Soft, adjustable fabric headband. Not a rigid helmet. Weight target: under 150g including all sensors.

## Pressure points to avoid

- Temporal bone contact with hard edges causes discomfort after 20 minutes
- Frontal electrodes require gentle spring pressure, not clamping force
- Bone conduction transducer position: just forward of the ear, not on the ear canal

## Electrode stability

Dry electrodes on the forehead require consistent contact pressure. A small foam backing behind each electrode helps maintain contact during minor head movements. Comb electrodes work better than flat pads for users with hair.

## Cable routing

Route all cables posteriorly to avoid dangling in the visual field. A single rear cable loom is preferable to multiple side exits.

## Heat management

The ESP32-S3 generates modest heat during active BLE streaming. Keep the compute module at the rear of the headband away from frontal sensing electrodes.

## 3D printing notes

PLA is acceptable for prototype enclosures. PETG is preferred for durability. Avoid rigid enclosures in contact with the skull — use flexible TPU inserts at contact points.
